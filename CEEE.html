Computer & Electrical Engineering Projects

Below are a compilation of my projects in the CE and EE space

Guitar Fuzz Pedal

My team at Columbia University designed, simulated, and implemented a guitar pedal to create a "fuzzy sound" iconic in indie and classic rock. The project's comprehensive report, full schematics, PCB view, simulation files, and even a 3D-printable casing if you get around to building it can be obtained in our repoository.

FPGA Music Box

I designed a module using Verilog and FPGAs to modify digital signals and output them as musical notes.

  • Learn More

GirasolEEG

Using an elementary electroencephelogram (EEG), I created a software library for using these devices to control the mouse on Linux/MacOS systems.

Guitar Pedal Project

This project was a final for Columbia University's ELEN1201 course. My team designed, simulated, and implemented a guitar effect pedal to recreate the "fuzzy" effect iconic to many indie songs nowadays.

GirasolEEG

This project was a directed alternate study under Deerfield Academy. During this project I studied the development and design of EEG devices and implemented my own software to pre-existing systems.

The future of brain computer interfaces (BCIs) are promising and novel advancements like these are trailblazers for the human input devices of the future.

FPGA Music Box

This project was a final assessment for Digital Logic and Computer Architecture (COM602) in Deerfield Academy. The project considered digital signals and their subsequent analog representations in audio. I had originally considered designing my own digital-to-analog, and vice versa, converter but that would be another project in and of it itself.
